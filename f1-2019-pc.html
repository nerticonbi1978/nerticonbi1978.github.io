In this F1 2019 Beginner’s guide, we will show you everything you need to know if you are just starting the game. This guide will be best for you if this is your first F1 game by Codemasters.

F1 2019 is a racing simulation game that is officially licensed by the FIA. Being a simulation game, it has many aspects of realism that gives the players the experience of a true Formula 1 driver.

F1 2019 Beginner’s Guide

Below we have detailed everything you need to know as a beginner in F1 2019.

Learn the Tracks

There are several tracks on the F1 calendar and the same goes with the game. From many high-speed corners and S turns, you have to understand how you should approach a corner and exit with the most speed you can carry. Formula 1 is not only about winning races but setting up competitive lap times that can rival your teammates or other drivers.

Use Time Trials mode to your advantage, as this mode gives you plenty of options, and different cars to try and understand a track. F1 2019 is not just a game where you can jump to a race without knowing the track. So get out there, and get a dozen or few laps in every track to understand the corners, how you should approach them, what gears you should be and where the DRS activation zones are for you to activate.

Driving Assists

Fortunately for new players, the game offers many driving assists that basically spoon feed everything from braking assists to counter steering assists. Beginners should have all assists on to understand the basic mechanics of the car.

Assists are not recommended when you get good enough on a track and have a better understanding. This is because it kills your ability to brake late and to accelerate earlier after exiting a corner. So its best to keep assists on when you are practicing a new track.

To learn the corners and braking zones of a track, you should activate the dynamic racing lines that can help you massively. These not only tell you where the racing lines are, but also indicates when you should be braking and applying the accelerator.

Once you get used to the car, the track, and are confident enough to take on tracks without assists. Turn off the most basic ones like counter steering, braking and accelerating assists. Keep Traction control and ABS on, as turning it off is only recommended for Intermediate level players.

How to Tackle Corners of a Track

F1 2019 Pc Gameplay

Any professional driver will always talk about keeping to the racing line, going in slow in a corner, and exiting fast. This holds very true in the game. the trick in being a great driver and setting fast lap times is to enter the corner from a wide angle, in a slow controlled speed, and then getting back on the power and using the full use of the curbs after exiting in speed.

You should also know when to brake. Most F1 cars require you to brake at the 100m mark, while corners that come after a long brake may require you to hit brakes as far as the 150m mark. Experiment with the braking zones, and learn where your car needs to be slowed done before you can comfortably go for the corner.

F1 cars are aerodynamic beasts when it comes to grip. Use this to your advantage and never be scared to point the nose of the car in the direction of the corner. You will be surprised how good the car responds in sharp turns. You can notice this when you take the last corner of Circuit Gilles Villeneuve track of the Canadian GP and just skim past the Wall of Champions.

Experiment Different Car Setups

There are many customization options for the cars in F1 2019. You can set up a car to your liking from having high downforce car for corners, or a less downforce car for long straight circuits.

As we said, there are many customization options that you can apply to your car, but the game makes it a bit easier for beginners to choose from 5 presets, along with a Custom preset for advanced customization.

What setup to decide for is all about player preference. But you should also be making obvious choices for the various tracks the game has. Not all tracks have long straights that require top speed. Tracks like Monaco require high grip to attack the corners, so you should choose the preset for high downforce. On the other hand, tracks like Baku, and Monza have the never-ending straights, which would require you to have a less downforce setup car for more maximum top speed.

Nonetheless, if you cannot find the comfortable balance with the presets, you can customize your own in the custom setup menu. This menu has everything from fuel load, aerodynamics, tire pressure, brakes, suspension, etc.

Race Weekends

If you start your F1 2019 Career mode, you will find out that the weekend starts with Practice sessions, followed by Qualifying sessions before the Race. Practice sessions are best to understand the track and to complete track programs.

Qualifying is all about getting in the highest position you can get to for the race start. IF you have done your prior training and know the track and your car, you can set up blistering lap times, and possibly score a pole position.

Race Starts and Overtaking

Formula 1 racing is all about getting ahead and gaining positions in a race. The competition is so high, that a good start off the line can guarantee you’re a couple of places gained or even lost. This is why you should know how to launch your car off the line.

It is best to use manual race start, as it gives you a clutch option. This not only guarantees a great take off but also eliminates chances of wheel spin. Wheel Spin is the worst thing you can have at the start, as you will launch much later, potentially losing a couple of positions instantly.

I found that using the manual race start with the clutch eliminated any wheel spin, and launched me good enough to gain at least 3 positions. While there is no training option for the clutch and the manual race starts in the game, you can only do these in races. So its best to learn the manual starts in random quick races.

Another big part of F1 2019 is overtaking. Formula 1 has two features that help the car overtake any race drivers ahead of them. The cars have Drag Reduction System (DRS) that allows you to overtake cars ahead in DRS activations Zones. The other thing is the Energy Recovery System (ERS) that is the power that is stored in your battery. This, in turn, gives you the extra boost you need to breeze past opponents for a clean overtake.

In conclusion, to get up to speed with F1 2019, you have to know the track, your car, and the basics of how to take a corner in the best way you can. Learn what features your car has and utilize everything that is in your hands when trying to gain positions. Experiment with your car setups to get an edge over other drivers, and don’t be embarrassed to use assists when trying to learn how to drive a Formula 1 car. Because we all have been there, and this is how you learn.
